### SEC EDGAR, Tickers, and CIKs: A Quick Guide

#### What is SEC EDGAR?
**EDGAR** (Electronic Data Gathering, Analysis, and Retrieval) is an automated system operated by the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). 
* **Purpose:** It allows public companies and foreign entities to electronically file required financial statements and disclosures (such as 10-K annual reports and 10-Q quarterly reports).
* **Access:** The platform is free and open to the public, making corporate financial data transparent for investors, analysts, and researchers.

#### Ticker vs. CIK: Tracking Companies
To pull data from EDGAR, you generally use one of two primary identifiers:

* **Ticker Symbol**
  * **Definition:** A short, unique alphanumeric code (typically 1 to 5 letters) assigned to a publicly traded company for trading purposes on a stock exchange.
  * **Example:** `AAPL` for Apple Inc., `MSFT` for Microsoft Corp.
  * **Key Characteristic:** Market-driven. Ticker symbols can change if a company moves to a different stock exchange or undergoes corporate rebranding.

* **CIK (Central Index Key)**
  * **Definition:** A unique, 10-digit number assigned by the SEC to every individual or company that files disclosures with them.
  * **Example:** Apple Inc.’s CIK is `0000320193`.
  * **Key Characteristic:** Legal/Regulatory identity. Unlike ticker symbols, a CIK is permanent and never changes, remaining tied to the legal entity for its entire existence.
